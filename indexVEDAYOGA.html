Principles of Yoga

The principles of yoga, also know as the 8 limbs, help navigate the path to self-realization. They are not hard fast rules but rather guidelines for living a more balanced, peaceful and meaningful life.

    Yama

    Moral Principles.

    Practices of purification that work specifically on the mind.

    Niyama

    Personal Discipline.

    Five observances that work on ways that we approach ourselves.

    Asana

    Physical Posture.

    Asanas are a gateway to the Self: they train the body to focus the mind.

    Pranayama

    Breath Control.

    The whirlpools of the mind can be stilled by the regulation of breath.

    Pratyahara

    Withdrawel of the Senses.

    Practice of further internalization that serves to quiet the mind.

    Dharna

    Concentration.

    The first stage of meditation is achieved by focusing the mind on a single point.

    Dhyana

    Meditation.

    A state we find ourselves in after much practice of inwardness and awareness.

    Samadhi

    Enlightenment.

    The perfection of yoga when the Self is realized.
